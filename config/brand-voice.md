# Brand Voice — Helmi Hasan

*This file is seeded with Helmi's core positioning and updated weekly by the Channel Analyst.*

## Who Helmi Is

YouTube strategist and consultant for service-based founders (coaches, consultants, agency owners).
He helps them use YouTube to attract inbound clients — without needing to go viral or post every day.

## Ideal Client Avatar (ICA)

- Service-based founder: coach, consultant, agency owner
- Earning $5K–$20K/month but growth feels stuck
- Already knows their niche — struggling with visibility and lead gen
- Tried content before (LinkedIn, IG) but no client results from it
- Wants inbound, not cold outreach
- Doesn't want to become a "content creator" — wants clients

## Tone

- Direct, no fluff
- Confident but not braggy
- Contrarian when the mainstream advice is wrong
- Story-led — uses his own founder journey as proof
- Malaysian-Australian voice: warm, real, occasionally self-deprecating
- Talks like a smart friend, not a guru

## Content Pillars

1. **YouTube as a client-acquisition tool** — not a vanity metric channel
2. **Founder-first strategy** — content that earns money, not just views
3. **The algorithm is not the enemy** — it rewards specificity
4. **Behind the scenes** — real numbers, real results, real process
5. **Debunking bad advice** — "you need to post every day" etc.

## Hooks That Work

- Contrarian opener: "Most YouTube advice is wrong for founders."
- Problem-first: "If you're not getting clients from your YouTube channel, here's why."
- Story opener: "I went from 0 to [X clients] in [Y months] — here's what actually moved the needle."
- Curiosity gap: "There's one thing killing founders' YouTube channels and nobody talks about it."

## Words Helmi Uses

- "founders" (not "entrepreneurs" or "business owners")
- "inbound clients" (not "leads")
- "your channel" (not "your brand")
- "the algorithm" (not "the platform")
- "what's working" (not "what's trending")
- "real" and "actually" — skepticism markers

## Words to Avoid

- "guru"
- "viral"
- "passive income"
- "hustle"
- "content creator" (for Helmi's ICA — they're not trying to be creators)
- Overly corporate: "leverage", "synergy", "utilize"

## Video Style

- Hook: 7-step framework (pattern interrupt → identify viewer → name problem → introduce opportunity → promise result → tease evidence → CTA to keep watching)
- Body: Story-insight hybrid — personal story anchors the main point
- Length: 8–15 minutes
- Pacing: Direct, no filler segments
